For ages, shopping has required an individual to physically reach a shop or market to purchase a product of their choice. With the advent of the internet and the penetration of computing devices; PC and Mobile, the shopping experience is slowly but steadily changing. Application programs that link the buyer and seller of a product have been developed. Such applications are powered by the internet and the internet enabled devices. With a click, a buyer with a product to purchase in mind is able to locate a seller and make a purchase of the product. The buyer can pay for the item online through the provided mode of payments such as Mobile Money, Debit and Credit cards or pay for the product in cash upon delivery. This mode of business has been referred to as e-commerce. e-Commerce, however, has mainly covered the buying and selling of manufactured and processed products. The ease of packaging and transportation plus the long shelf life has ensured these have an edge over farm produce. This has left out the shoppers and sellers of farm produce such as vegetables, fruits and animal products. These have had to endure the challenges that online shopping helps to alleviate. Transportation challenges such as financial cost and traffic congestion in the cities with implications of time lost. Others include dealing with large crowds in the market, petty theft, foul language and Mob and unscrupulous sellers who occasional sell at higher than reasonable prices for the produce. With the “Fresh Food Online Market”, buyers will be linked to sellers in different markets of fresh foods. These will have the benefit of competitive prices as they will be able to compare prices of different sellers in different markets. This will equally expose sellers to a larger market of buyers online. The increased sales will translate into increased incomes. Buyers will have convenience in shopping as they will save time by not having to reach the markets physically. Similarly, the crowds, petty theft, the foul language and unscrupulous sellers will be avoided due to the elimination of the physical necessity to reach the markets to transact.
